## Overview

**Remote viewing** is the claimed psychic ability to perceive or describe details about distant or unseen locations, objects, or events through extrasensory perception (ESP). The practice gained significant attention during the Cold War when the U.S. government funded research programs investigating whether remote viewing could be used for intelligence gathering.

The most famous of these programs, the **Stargate Project**, ran from the 1970s through 1995 and cost approximately $20 million. The program was declassified in 1995, along with an independent evaluation by the American Institutes for Research (AIR) that concluded remote viewing showed no evidence of being a reliable or useful intelligence tool.

**Scientific consensus** finds no credible evidence for remote viewing beyond what would be expected by chance. Controlled studies have consistently failed to demonstrate the phenomenon under rigorous conditions, and methodological flaws have been identified in studies claiming positive results.

## Remote Viewing Protocols

### Coordinate Remote Viewing (CRV)

**Developer**: Ingo Swann (1970s)
**Usage**: Primary military protocol

A structured six-stage protocol developed for the Stargate Project:

| Stage | Focus | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Stage 1 | Ideograms | Initial impressions and basic gestalts |
| Stage 2 | Sensory Data | Colors, textures, temperatures, sounds |
| Stage 3 | Dimensional Data | Sketches, measurements, spatial relationships |
| Stage 4 | Emotional Impact | Feelings, aesthetics, intangibles |
| Stage 5 | Detailed Analysis | In-depth target analysis |
| Stage 6 | 3D Modeling | Three-dimensional target representation |

### Extended Remote Viewing (ERV)

**Method**: Altered states of consciousness
**Characteristics**: 
- Deep relaxation techniques
- Theta brainwave state induction
- Free-form perception reporting
- Similar to meditative states

### Written Remote Viewing (WRV)

**Method**: Simplified protocol for beginners
**Characteristics**:
- Target coordinates provided
- Free-form written descriptions
- Sketches encouraged
- Less structured than CRV

### Methodology Components

All protocols typically include:
- **Double-blind protocol**: Viewer should not know target identity
- **Monitor role**: Records viewer impressions without feedback
- **Feedback stage**: Target revealed after session completion
- **Independent judging**: Third parties match descriptions to targets

## The Stargate Project

### Overview

The **Stargate Project** was the umbrella term for a series of U.S. government programs investigating remote viewing and other psychic phenomena for potential intelligence applications.

### Program Names (Chronological)

The program operated under multiple code names:
- **SCANATE** (1972-1973) — Early CIA-sponsored research
- **GONDOLA WISH** (1977-1979) — Army Intelligence program
- **GRILL FLAME** (1979-1983) — Operational remote viewing unit
- **CENTER LANE** (1983-1985) — Continuation under new name
- **SUN STREAK** (1985-1990) — DIA-managed program
- **STAR GATE** (1990-1995) — Final program name

### Key Facts

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Duration** | 1970s - 1995 (approximately 23 years) |
| **Total Funding** | ~$20 million over program lifetime |
| **Primary Agencies** | CIA, DIA, U.S. Army Intelligence |
| **Personnel** | Approximately 20-40 remote viewers and support staff |
| **Purpose** | Intelligence gathering and target identification |
| **Declassification** | 1995 |
| **Outcome** | Program terminated; no operational value found |

### Program Conclusion

In 1995, the CIA commissioned the **American Institutes for Research (AIR)** to conduct an independent evaluation of the Stargate Project. Key findings:

> ❌ **No Reliable Demonstration**: The research did not demonstrate remote viewing under controlled conditions that could rule out chance explanations.

> ❌ **No Actionable Intelligence**: No evidence was found that remote viewing produced actionable intelligence.

> ❌ **Methodological Flaws**: Studies claiming positive results were found to have significant methodological problems.

> ❌ **No Operational Value**: The evaluation concluded the program had no practical intelligence value.

**Result**: The CIA terminated the program and declassified the documents in 1995.

## Scientific Assessment

### Mainstream Scientific Consensus

> ⚠️ **Scientific Consensus**: The overwhelming consensus in the scientific community is that there is **no credible evidence** for remote viewing beyond what would be expected by chance.

### Methodological Issues Identified

Studies claiming to demonstrate remote viewing have been criticized for:

1. **Inadequate Controls**
   - Insufficient double-blind protocols
   - Sensory leakage and subtle cueing
   - Poor isolation of viewers from target information

2. **Subjective Evaluation**
   - Vague descriptions that could fit multiple targets
   - Subjective judging criteria
   - Confirmation bias in matching descriptions to targets

3. **Statistical Problems**
   - Selective reporting of "hits"
   - File drawer effect (unpublished negative results)
   - Multiple comparison problems
   - Post-hoc rationalization

4. **Replication Failures**
   - Independent researchers unable to replicate positive results
   - Effect size diminishes with stricter controls
   - Results consistent with chance under rigorous conditions

### Theoretical Problems

Remote viewing faces fundamental theoretical challenges:

- **No Known Mechanism**: No physical or biological mechanism for information transfer
- **Violates Physics**: Inconsistent with established physical laws
- **No Distance Effect**: Claims show no correlation with distance (violates inverse square law)
- **Information Theory**: No explanation for how information is encoded/decoded
- **Lack of Framework**: No coherent theoretical framework

### Skeptical Explanations

Alternative explanations for claimed successes:

| Explanation | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Cold Reading** | Mentalist techniques for appearing psychic |
| **Sensory Leakage** | Subtle cues from monitors or environment |
| **Confirmation Bias** | Selective attention to hits, ignoring misses |
| **Vague Descriptions** | General statements retrofitted to targets |
| **Statistical Artifacts** | Misunderstanding of probability and chance |
| **Selective Memory** | Remembering hits, forgetting misses |

### Why Claims Are Problematic

1. **Lack of Verification**: No independent confirmation of successes
2. **Confirmation Bias**: Hits remembered, misses forgotten
3. **Existing Intelligence**: "Hits" could be based on conventional intelligence
4. **Vague Predictions**: Descriptions general enough to fit multiple targets
5. **Selective Reporting**: Only successes publicized, failures buried

## Problems with Remote Viewing Research

### File Drawer Effect
Negative results often go unpublished while positive results are publicized, creating false impression of efficacy.

### Sensory Leakage
Viewers may receive subtle cues from monitors, environment, or target selection process.

### Subjective Judging
Matching descriptions to targets involves subjective judgment prone to confirmation bias.

### Selective Reporting
"Hits" are emphasized while misses are explained away or forgotten.

### Vague Descriptions
Impressions are often general enough to match multiple targets.

### Statistical Misunderstanding
Coincidences and chance results misinterpreted as evidence for remote viewing.

❓ **Scientific Acceptance**: Peer-reviewed research would support the phenomenon

## Conclusion

Remote viewing remains **unverified** by scientific standards. While the U.S. government invested over two decades and approximately $20 million investigating the phenomenon, the official conclusion was that remote viewing had **no practical intelligence value**.

The American Institutes for Research evaluation found no compelling evidence for the phenomenon, and mainstream science identifies significant methodological flaws in studies claiming positive results.

**Key Takeaways**:
- ❌ No credible scientific evidence beyond chance
- ❌ Government program concluded it had no operational value
- ❌ Replication failures under controlled conditions
- ❌ Methodological flaws in supporting research
- ❌ No theoretical framework consistent with known physics

While remote viewing maintains a following in popular culture and commercial circles, it lacks the scientific foundation necessary for acceptance as a genuine phenomenon.
